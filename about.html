About Me

I was born and raised in NE Ohio. I remained there until after graduating from college (or university) when I entered into the first of 12 years of total active duty service in the Army. In 2011, after a few cross-country moves and two long tours in Afghanistan I left active duty for the first time only to head back to Afghanistan for a year as an intelligence analyst.

After that year (and some serious soul searching) I moved to Budapest, Hungary for two years and earned an MBA at Corvinus University of Budapest. While immersing myself in Hungarian culture and traveling extensively I somehow managed to excel academically, which included my thesis on the effects of a positive work environment on people suffering with mental illness. Which is exactly what I did.

Upon graduation in the summer of 2015 I moved to Los Angeles to start a small company, Logos Products, employing two recently homeless veterans and a fashion designer to make bags. We raised $25k in pre-orders for our tote bag, duffel, backpack, and fannypack through a crowdfunding campaign. The three of them spent the next year fulfilling these pre-orders while I tried (and failed) to generate opportunities to sell our products in the future. I severely miscalculated the barriers to entry of the LA farmers market scene which was one of the critical failures of Logos. So like all failed entrepreneurs I chose to pivot from a company with long-term prospects to a short-term project focused on setting up our employees for whatever came next for them. All in all, it was a financial disaster but a social success.

Following the closure of Logos I went back onto active duty with the Army in 2017 and spent the next four years working unique assignments in Germany, Turkey, Jordan and UK. Following those four years I returned home to Cleveland, Ohio and began the pursuing my education in graphic and web design at Tri-C.

In addition to geeking out on all things design I enjoy foods from across the world, French pressed coffee, long-distance running and traveling whenever I can. I've also volunteered for the U.S. Committee for Refugees and Immigrants (USCRI) Cleveland Field Office.
